Faq's

Fire ecology is the study of how fire interacts with the natural environment, including the relationships between fire, plants, animals, and ecosystems. It encompasses the processes of fire ignition, behavior, and effects on ecosystems, as well as the adaptations of organisms to fire.

Some ecosystems, like grasslands and certain types of forests, have evolved with frequent fires. Fire can clear out vegetation, release nutrients, and promote the growth of fire-adapted species.

Plants in fire-prone ecosystems have developed adaptations such as thick bark, fire-resistant seeds, and the ability to resprout after a fire.

Fire can affect nutrient cycling, soil properties, plant community composition, and wildlife habitat. It can also promote biodiversity by creating diverse habitats.

Many animals have developed behaviors to escape or avoid fires. Some species may benefit from post-fire habitat, while others may be negatively affected due to habitat loss.
